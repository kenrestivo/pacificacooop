“Surf’s Up at Co-op Fundraiser”

by Jonathan Villar

With the surf trade helping the local economy, and a tight-knit local crew, it’s no surprise when surfers themselves contribute for the good of the community.

For the second year in a row, local surfing entrepreneurs have donated a collectors-grade surfboard to Pacifica Co-op Nursery School for its annual Springfest fundraiser.

The surfers’ contribution came as no surprise to Springfest Co-chair Lauri Vreeland. “Well, the Pedro Point Surf Club, they have their annual scholarship,” she said. “There are attorneys who surf. There are a lot of artists in the water; it’s such a broad spectrum of life. They (surfers) do a lot for this community and a lot of people don’t know about that.”

Local professional surfer Matt Ambrose and his wife Leigh Ann, Co-op parents, spearheaded the first surfboard raffle last year. Neptune Surfboards co-owner Brent Hudson donated a ten-foot surfboard, which is called a “gun,” he’d designed to surf Maverick’s, the world-famous big-wave break in Half Moon Bay.

The board was glassed by Randy Cone and decorated by local artist Kevin Ancell, who is world-renowned for his work with various media ranging from movie set design and sculpture to oil painting and building art surfboards.

Ambrose was going to shape the first board for the raffle, but it turned out that Hudson had shaped a surfboard blank too thin for his own use.

In past years, the Co-op raffle prize was a quilt crafted by a group of parents. “But last year Leigh Ann thought of having Matt and herself put together a board for the raffle,” said Co-op President Eileen Manning-Villar. “It was a very successful fundraiser for the school.”

The surfboard raffle is fast becoming a grand draw for the fundraiser. “It’s taken it up a notch from the quilt—I think that was a beautiful thing too,” Vreeland said. “It’s really accelerated the event and included a lot more of the community.”

This year, Matt Ambrose has collaborated again with Ancell, purchasing a balsa wood longboard from Santa Barbara shaper Reynolds Yater. “He’s been around forever. He used to shape for Velzy and he was a big part of the shortboard revolution.” Sticking to an ocean theme, Ancell adorned the deck with a mermaid from shiny Mother-of-Pearl inlay, with flowing hair made of gold leaf and India ink, glassing it with a shiny gloss coat.

“If I was selling this board, it would go for at least two or three grand,” Ancell said.

If the Mavericks Surf Contest happens this year, Manning-Villar said, she hopes to accompany Leigh Ann, and a few other Co-op parents to display the board at the Pillar Point contest site.

The contest, which is held if the waves reach at least 20-25 feet, draws thousands of spectators who line the cliffs to watch the invitees challenge the powerful surf. Ancell created the trophies for the contest, and Matt, part of the first crew of locals to regularly surf the break with Jeff Clark in the early nineties, is always a contender in the contest.

The board will be displayed at Salada Beach Café through the month of February. After that it can be viewed at NorCal Surf Shop and it is scheduled to make appearances at Linda Mar Safeway, Pacifica Farmer’s Market, Kerry’s Coffee Shop, Chit Chat Café, Colombo’s Deli, Pacifica Mothers’ Club, Oceana High School and Terra Nova High School.

“We’re excited that the Ambrose Family is involved again this year,” Vreeland said. “This is such a surfing community and it’s really a draw at the Springfest event.”

Proceeds from Springfest will help keep tuition costs down at the 44-year old school. It will also go toward the ever necessary capital improvements and routine maintenance.

Springfest 2005 will be held on Saturday, March 19, from 6:30 to 10:30 p.m. at the Pacifica Community Center, 540 Crespi Drive. It will include silent and live auctions, wine tasting, and hearty hors d’oeuvres in addition to the surfboard raffle. Tickets for the event are $25 and attendees must be at least 21.
